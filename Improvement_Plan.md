### 3. CRITICAL OBSERVATIONS
*   **Success Model:** The Computer Science (CSE) department has set the institutional benchmark at 71.4%. High attendance and proactive internal monitoring are contributing factors.
*   **High-Risk Zones:** Electrical (EEE) and Electronics (ECE) branches are performing significantly below the institutional average.
*   **Lab Proficiency:** 100% success in labs suggests practical understanding is strong, but translation to theoretical long-form answers in External Exams is the primary bottleneck.

### 4. STRATEGIC INTERVENTION PLAN (SEM-II)

#### A. Remedial Instruction (Project 'Lift')
*   **Subject Focus:** Mandatory zero-hour remedial classes for "Electrical Circuit Analysis" and "Electronic Devices" (Impact areas for EEE/ECE).
*   **Frequency:** 4 days a week, 4:00 PM – 5:00 PM.

#### B. Peer-to-Peer Academic Mentoring
*   **Model:** Pair the top 10% students from CSE (Benchmark Branch) with students from EEE and ECE for group study sessions.
*   **Incentive:** Mentors receive "Academic Excellence Certifications."

#### C. Assessment & Evaluation Changes
*   **Biannual Mock Externals:** Conduct full-syllabus mock exams 15 days before the final university examinations.
*   **Flash-Card Learning:** Implementation of quick-recall sessions for definitions and formulae in every morning session.

1.  **Overall Pass Rate Target:** **75%** (A +11.37% increase)
